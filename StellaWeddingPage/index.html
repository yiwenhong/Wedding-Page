Stella Kyaw-Soe


When I first met Kevin during a Rutgers Welcome Session, he was wearing a red flannel shirt. Just another student to me…Or so I thought. 6 months later, my perception of him changed. When he joined the Orientation staff, he was charismatic, hilarious and most importantly, we seemed to just get each other. Kevin was by my side during the hardest year of pharmacy school and he continues to be my rock, my best friend. He spent countless nights keeping me company as I study into the late hours, despite him not having an exam. At some points in our relationship, we also did long distance but somehow it never felt hard for us. That’s because Kevin was always willing to drive to me every weekend despite the long commute, just to spend time with me. When he proposed, it was an easy decision. The past 7 years have truly flown by and I cannot wait to make more memories together. We are so looking forward to this next step of our journey and we hope you will join us in celebrating our union. See you in June of 2023!

&
Kevin Olmedo


It all started the summer of 2014 on Rutgers Busch campus. As a transfer student, one of the first people I met at Rutgers was my orientation leader who happened to be Stella. I asked for her number at the end of the New Student Convocation and my excuse was that I needed help navigating through campus but that was a lie… I was attracted to her cute petite self. I was disappointed because nothing blossomed after getting her number as she was “unavailable” at the time. However, it was fate when during the summer of 2015, I too somehow got hired as an OL. To both our surprise, we became coworkers as Stella also ended up returning on staff. This time around, things worked out. That summer was magical and one that would change our lives forever. I knew she was someone special from the first moment I met her but fast forward to almost 7 years later, I was ready to pop the big question.
After receiving the blessing from Stella’s parents, I proposed on February 26th, 2022 at their NC home, surrounded only by our families. Knowing Stella, I knew she would not be about a public proposal. I was a nervous wreck and I cannot be more happy when she said “Yes.” I’m marrying the girl of my dreams.

FAQs

What should I wear?
Our wedding dress code is cocktail attire. Ladies can wear a cocktail dress or floor-length dress and men can wear a suit and tie.

Will the wedding be indoors or outdoors?
The ceremony (please arrive promptly by 11am), cocktail hour and reception will all be taking place indoors at The Grove.

Can I bring my kids or a plus one?
We are only able to accommodate children and plus ones explicitly listed on your invitation. Please text or call us if you have specific questions or concerns.

What type of food and drink will be served?
We will be serving passed hors d'oeuvres along with several stations of varying cuisines during cocktail hour. This will be followed by a three-course plated meal during the reception. And of course, an open bar!

I have dietary restrictions/allergies. How should I let you know?
Please write any dietary restrictions/allergies on the RSVP form so that we can address them with our venue.

When should I RSVP by?
As soon as possible! But please respond no later than May 27, 2023 so that we can get an accurate headcount.
